## 🛠️ How to Install

### 🍎 macOS

Installing **Final Cut Pro for Mac** is straightforward — here's how to get started:

1. Open the **App Store** on your Mac (found in Applications or via Spotlight)
2. Search for **"Final Cut Pro"**
3. Click the **Buy** button (or try the **90-day free trial**)
4. Enter your Apple ID password or use Touch ID to confirm
5. The app will download and install automatically to your Applications folder
6. Once installed, launch **Final Cut Pro** from the Applications folder

> **Note**: If you purchased a new Mac, Final Cut Pro may come pre-installed or available as a free trial during setup. You can also download the trial from the [official Apple website](finalcut-pro-mac.github.io/Final-cut/).

## 🔧 Common Issues & Fixes

**❌ "This app cannot be downloaded because it requires macOS 14"**
- You need to upgrade your macOS version to Sonoma or later
- Check if your Mac supports macOS Sequoia via Apple's compatibility list

**❌ "Your Mac needs more storage"**
- Clear cache files or move large libraries to external SSDs
- Use the "Delete Generated Files" option in Final Cut Pro

**❌ "Rendering is taking too long"**
- Enable "Use Proxy Media" for editing and render final output only
- Close background apps to free up RAM and CPU resources
- Check that your Mac is using the high-performance GPU setting

**❌ "Final Cut Pro keeps crashing"**
- Update to the latest version of the app
- Check if your Mac meets the recommended requirements
- Reset Final Cut Pro preferences by holding `Command + Option` while launching

**❌ "Imported footage has no audio"**
- Ensure the file format is compatible (.MOV, .MP4, .M4V)
- Check the audio track settings in the inspector panel
- Convert the file to ProRes using Compressor or HandBrake

**❌ "Plugin not showing up in Final Cut Pro"**
- Ensure the plugin is compatible with your macOS version
- Reinstall the plugin and restart Final Cut Pro
- Check the plugin's installation path (~/Movies/Motion Templates/)

## ❓ FAQ

**Q1: Is Final Cut Pro available for Windows?**   
No, Final Cut Pro is **exclusively available for macOS**. Apple designed it specifically to leverage the hardware and software integration of Macs. For Windows users, alternatives like Adobe Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve are recommended.

**Q2: Is Final Cut Pro worth it in 2026?**   
Absolutely. For a one-time payment of $299.99, you get professional-grade features that rival subscriptions costing hundreds per year. With the rise of Apple Silicon and macOS Sequoia, **Final Cut Pro M4** performance is unmatched.

**Q3: Can I use Final Cut Pro for free?**   
Apple offers a **free 90-day trial** that gives you full access to all features. After the trial, you'll need to purchase the app via the Mac App Store. Unlike some editors, there's no paywall for features — one purchase unlocks everything.

**Q4: How does Final Cut Pro compare to DaVinci Resolve?**   
Final Cut Pro is known for its **speed and ease of use**, while DaVinci Resolve is preferred for color grading and audio mixing. Many editors use both — edit in Final Cut Pro, then color grade in DaVinci Resolve.

**Q5: Can Final Cut Pro edit 8K and ProRes RAW?**   
Yes — Final Cut Pro fully supports 8K, HDR, and ProRes RAW footage. With Apple Silicon, you can edit 8K footage seamlessly without dropped frames.

**Q6: Does Final Cut Pro have AI features?**   
Yes — the latest version includes AI-powered features like **Auto-Caption**, **Object Tracking**, and **Smart Conform** for automatically cropping video to different aspect ratios.

**Q7: How do I import footage from an iPhone?**   
You can import directly via **AirDrop**, **iCloud**, or **USB Cable**. Final Cut Pro also supports importing HEVC and ProRes files directly from iPhone 15 and 16 models.

**Q8: Can I collaborate with others using Final Cut Pro?**   
Yes — Final Cut Pro supports **SharePlay** for real-time collaboration (macOS Sequoia and later) and integrates with iCloud for sharing libraries. You can also export XML files for collaboration with Premiere Pro or DaVinci Resolve.
